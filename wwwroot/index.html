Meet The Team

Donald G. Hooie, DDS

Dr. Hooie graduated with honors from the University of Tennessee College of Dentistry. Each year, Dr. Hooie continues his education with some of the most well-known clinicians in the country focusing on teeth-whitening, dentures, implants, crowns, dental bridges, tooth-colored fillings, non-surgical gum care, single-visit root canal therapy, laser dentistry, and adult orthodontics.

Dr. Hooie's memberships include the American Dental Association, the Tennessee Dental Association, the Crossville Dental Society, adn the Association of Military Surgeons. He is heighly-involved in helping those without access to qulity dental and is a volunteer dentist at Remote Area Medical, the Navajo Indian Reservation, the Kuna Indian Reservation, and in Panama.

When not in the offic, Dr. Hooie holds multiple positions at First united Methodist Church, including playin the bas with the praise band, Boy Scouts, and Crossville Lions Club, where he is a past president. He enjoys canoeing, kayaking, climbing/rappelling, and family time.

Dental Restorations

If you have a cracked, chipped, infected, or damaged tooth, please give us a call to make an appointment. Crowns, veneers, tooth-colored porcelain fillings, and bonding are common techniques for dental restoration. We will recommend the best option for your case to restore your tooth to its full functionality and give you the beautiful smile you deserve.

Crowns & Bridges

Protect and keep badly decayed or fractured teeth while maintaining a beautiful smile. Made to perfectly match the shape and shade of your other teeth, they’re durable, strong, and undetectable.

Crowns should be placed before the tooth is so decayed that it may fracture. This can often help prevent the expense of root canal therapy in the future. If a tooth is too damaged to have a crown applied, a porcelain bridge may be a good alternative.

Implant Restorations

Dental implants are a great option for replacing missing teeth, improving chewing ability, eliminating bridges, and replacing dentures. Several implants may provide a base for a series of artificial teeth known as a fixed bridge. Implants can even be used to secure a full set of removable dentures for people who have no remaining natural teeth.

Veneers & Laminates

Veneers and laminates are very thin porcelain pieces that are custom made to adhere directly to the front of teeth without grinding or shaving. Transform your smile without drilling on your natural teeth!

In the hands of a highly skilled dentist, porcelain laminates and veneers can provide you with an incredibly natural, beautiful smile. They are virtually undetectable and can dramatically improve your appearance. They are durable and long-lasting when compared to other cosmetic treatments.

Dentures & Partials

Do you wear old dentures or have a mouthful of missing or failing teeth? Eat what you want again, smile, laugh, and look and feel years younger with our expert-crafted dentures and partial dentures. If you have natural teeth in place, you can't wear a full-set of dentures, so we recommend partial dentures. Full dentures can be made to replace all of your teeth in the event that this is necessary. Dentures replace teeth in a non-invasive way without the need for surgery.

Smile-Whitening

Many everyday things like coffee, tea and wine can significantly dull teeth. Smile-whitening enables us to significantly brighten your smile for that dazzling white smile you deserve.

Plastic whitening trays will be made from models of your teeth. You will then place a special whitening gel in each tray and wear the trays in your mouth for several hours per day. (Many patients wear their trays after dinner and before bed.) A significant change in tooth color is usually seen in just four to six weeks.

Gum Contouring

Even, well-proportioned gums are the key to a beautiful smile. The procedure is simple and can often be done without the need for a numbing agent thanks to laser dentistry. By removing excess gum tissue that is covering your front teeth making them appear small and non-uniform, we can give your "gummy" smile the beautiful, natural look you deserve.

Periodontal Therapy

Gum disease is treated by carefully removing the bacteria and substances that form in the pockets around the teeth. The removal of this material occurs on a microscopic level and requires great skill. Our dental team has had advanced training to effectively remove all of the bacteria.
